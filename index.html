Analyzed Techniques

The research focuses on the evolution from GAN-based warping methods to modern Latent Diffusion Models (LDMs). Below are the primary techniques extracted from the provided literature.

CP-VTON

GAN-Based

Mechanism: Characteristic-Preserving Image-based VTON. Uses Geometric Matching Module (GMM) for warping and Try-On Module (TOM) for blending.

Year: 2018 Baseline

VITON-HD

GAN (High-Res)

Mechanism: Focuses on high-resolution (1024x768) generation via Misalignment-Aware Normalization (ALIGN) to handle warping artifacts.

Year: 2021 High Fidelity

CatVTON

Diffusion (Lightweight)

Mechanism: "Concatenation is All You Need". Simplifies diffusion input by concatenating latent representations. Efficient training.

Year: 2024 Efficiency

Voost

Transformer Diffusion

Mechanism: Unified scalable diffusion transformer for bidirectional Virtual Try-On and Try-Off. Handles diverse poses and lighting.

Year: 2025 Unified

OmniTry

Mask-Free Diffusion

Mechanism: Generalizes to any wearable object (jewelry, bags) without explicit masks. Uses inpainting repurposing.

Year: 2025 Versatility

FITMI

Latent Textual Inv.

Mechanism: Enhanced Latent Diffusion Textual Inversion. Focuses on realistic texture preservation and user interaction.

Year: 2025 Text-Guided

Efficiency & Complexity

Output Quality & Evaluation Metrics

Quantitative evaluation relies on FID (Fr√©chet Inception Distance) for realism (lower is better) and SSIM (Structural Similarity Index) for structural fidelity (higher is better). Recent diffusion models (Voost, CatVTON) achieve significantly lower FID scores than earlier GAN approaches.

Metric Comparison (Normalized)

  • FID: Lower values indicate better perceptual quality.
  • SSIM: Higher values indicate better structural preservation.
  • LPIPS: Lower values indicate better perceptual similarity to ground truth.

Key Research Finding

Diffusion models (CatVTON, Voost) represent a paradigm shift. Unlike GANs which often struggle with large occlusions (e.g., arms crossed over chest), diffusion models "inpain" the missing areas more naturally, resulting in fewer geometric artifacts and better background preservation.
